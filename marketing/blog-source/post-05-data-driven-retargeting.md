## What is Data-Driven Retargeting?

Data-driven retargeting uses visitor behavior, intent signals, and profile data to create personalized campaigns that re-engage people who've shown interest in your product or service.

Unlike basic retargeting that shows the same ad to everyone who visited your site, data-driven approaches segment audiences based on:

- **Pages viewed** (pricing page visitors vs. blog readers)
- **Time on site** (30 seconds vs. 5 minutes)
- **Return visits** (first-time vs. recurring visitors)
- **Intent signals** (actively researching solutions like yours)
- **Firmographic data** (company size, industry, revenue)
- **Engagement level** (scroll depth, video watches, downloads)

### Step 2: Segment by Intent and Fit

Not all visitors are equal. Segment your retargeting audiences by:

**High Intent + High Fit:**
- Viewed pricing or demo pages
- Spent 5+ minutes on product pages
- Match your ideal customer profile
- **Retargeting strategy:** Aggressive direct response ads, personalized demo offers, sales outreach

**High Intent + Low Fit:**
- Engaged deeply but don't match ICP
- Students, competitors, wrong company size
- **Retargeting strategy:** Educational content, nurture sequences, low-cost awareness

**Low Intent + High Fit:**
- Quick blog visit from target account
- High-value company, minimal engagement
- **Retargeting strategy:** Thought leadership content, case studies, problem-focused messaging

**Low Intent + Low Fit:**
- Exclude from retargeting to save budget
- Or create separate low-cost awareness campaign

Using behavioral segmentation tools, you can automatically create these segments and activate them across email, ads, and direct mail channels—all from one platform.

The best retargeting happens automatically based on visitor behavior:

## Measuring Retargeting ROI: Metrics That Matter

Vanity metrics don't pay the bills. Track these instead:

**Attribution Metrics:**
- **View-through conversions:** Did they see your ad before converting?
- **Multi-touch attribution:** Which touchpoints contributed to the sale?
- **Time to conversion:** How long from first visit to close?

**Audience Quality Metrics:**
- **Match rate:** What % of visitors were identified and retargetable?
- **ICP fit score:** How many retargeting leads match your ideal customer?
- **Intent score:** Are you retargeting active buyers or casual browsers?

**Efficiency Metrics:**
- **Cost per qualified lead:** Not just any lead—qualified leads
- **Return on ad spend (ROAS):** Revenue generated / ad spend
- **Customer acquisition cost (CAC):** Total marketing cost / new customers

**Engagement Metrics:**
- **Click-through rate (CTR):** Are people engaging with your ads?
- **Conversion rate by segment:** Which segments convert best?
- **Frequency:** How many times did someone see your ad before converting?

## Common Retargeting Mistakes to Avoid

### Retargeting Everyone the Same Way

**The mistake:** Showing identical ads to blog readers and pricing page visitors.

**The fix:** Create separate campaigns for each intent level. Blog readers get educational content. Pricing visitors get demo offers.

### Burning Budget on Low-Intent Visitors

**The mistake:** Retargeting everyone who visits your site, including competitors, students, and job seekers.

**The fix:** Use firmographic filters to exclude non-ICP visitors. Focus budget on high-fit prospects.

### Ignoring Frequency Caps

**The mistake:** Showing your ad 50 times to the same person (hello, ad fatigue).

**The fix:** Set frequency caps at 5-7 impressions per week. If they haven't converted after 20 impressions, they're not interested.

### Stopping Too Soon

**The mistake:** Ending retargeting campaigns after 7 days because you didn't see immediate results.

**The fix:** B2B buying cycles are 3-9 months. Keep retargeting (at lower frequency) for 60-90 days.

### Using Only One Channel

**The mistake:** Running Google ads only, missing opportunities in email, LinkedIn, and direct mail.

**The fix:** Create multi-channel sequences. Display ads + email + direct mail converts 3-5x better than single-channel retargeting.

### Not Testing Creative

**The mistake:** Running the same ad for months without testing variations.

**The fix:** Test headlines, offers, CTAs, and imagery. Swap out underperforming ads every 2-3 weeks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is data-driven retargeting?

Data-driven retargeting uses visitor behavior, intent signals, and profile data to create personalized campaigns that re-engage prospects across multiple channels. Instead of showing the same ad to everyone, you segment audiences based on pages viewed, engagement level, company fit, and buying intent.

### How does retargeting differ from remarketing?

The terms are often used interchangeably, but technically retargeting refers to paid ad campaigns (display, social, video) while remarketing typically refers to email campaigns. Both aim to re-engage people who've shown interest in your product.

### What's a good retargeting conversion rate?

Industry benchmarks vary, but B2B retargeting campaigns typically see 0.5-2% conversion rates for cold traffic and 5-10% for warm traffic (pricing page visitors, demo requests). Data-driven segmentation can push these 2-3x higher.

### How much does retargeting cost?

Retargeting CPCs (cost per click) range from $0.50-$5 depending on platform and audience. LinkedIn is most expensive ($3-$8 CPC) but often highest quality for B2B. Google Display and Meta are cheaper ($0.50-$2 CPC) but require more volume.

### How long should I run retargeting campaigns?

For B2B products, run campaigns for at least 60-90 days since buying cycles are long. For B2C or lower-cost products, 14-30 days is typical. Use frequency caps to avoid ad fatigue, and reduce ad spend after 30 days for visitors who haven't engaged.

### Can I retarget anonymous website visitors?

Yes—that's exactly what visitor identification enables. Cursive reveals up to 70% of anonymous traffic, providing company names, contact information, and firmographic data so you can retarget visitors even if they never filled out a form.

### What platforms are best for B2B retargeting?

LinkedIn offers the highest-quality B2B targeting but at premium prices. Google Display Network provides massive reach at lower cost. Facebook/Instagram work well for SMB audiences. The best approach uses multiple platforms simultaneously to create multi-touchpoint campaigns.

### How do I avoid annoying people with retargeting ads?

Set frequency caps (5-7 impressions per week maximum), vary your creative regularly, use sequential messaging instead of repeating the same ad, and exclude people after 20+ impressions if they haven't converted. Also exclude existing customers unless running upsell campaigns.
